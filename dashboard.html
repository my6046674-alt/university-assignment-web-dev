    Top 5 Screen Time Management Strategies

    1. Set Clear Limits

    Use built-in device features or apps to set daily screen time limits. Aim for balanced usage, prioritizing educational screens over entertainment.

    2. Pomodoro Technique

    Study for 25-50 minutes focused sessions followed by short breaks. During breaks, step away from screens to reduce eye strain.

    3. Do Not Disturb Mode

    Turn off notifications during study hours to avoid distractions from social media and messages.

    4. Track Usage

    Regularly check screen time reports to identify high-usage apps and adjust habits accordingly.

    5. Screen-Free Zones

    Designate study areas or meal times as screen-free to promote focus and better sleep.

    Common Time Management Mistakes

    Procrastination

    Waiting until the last minute to start assignments, leading to rushed work and high stress.

    Poor Planning

    Not breaking down long-term projects into smaller steps or scheduling them properly.

    Multitasking

    Trying to do multiple tasks at once, which reduces efficiency and increases errors.

    Overcommitting

    Saying yes to too many activities, leaving no time for rest or deep focus.

    Ignoring Fatigue

    Studying when overly tired without taking breaks, leading to poor retention.

    Dealing with Study Fatigue

    Problem: Getting tired while studying leads to procrastination and reduced focus. Reading dense material when fatigued makes it hard to concentrate.

    Peak Energy Times

    Study during your peak energy times (e.g., mornings or after a short rest).

    Take Breaks

    Take frequent short breaks: Walk around, stretch, or jog in place for a few minutes.

    Power Naps

    Avoid long naps mid-session; opt for power naps (10-20 minutes) if needed.

    Stay Healthy

    Stay hydrated, eat healthy snacks, and ensure good sleep hygiene at night.

    Active Learning

    Use active study techniques like teaching the material aloud to stay engaged.
